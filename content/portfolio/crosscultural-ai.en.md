## Project Overview

**Duration**: 2024.09 — 2025.07  
**Type**: Master's Thesis & Survey Project  
**Methods**: In-depth interviews, digital behavior tracking, participant observation, longitudinal study

## Core Work

- Led longitudinal tracking study of **30 core users**, executing **50+ in-depth interviews** and multi-round digital behavior monitoring
- Conducted long-term observation and analysis of AI usage habits in non-native language environments
- Focused on capturing "non-verbal signals" such as silence and avoidance during interactions
- Established interaction need assessment dimensions for neurodiverse users (ADHD, social & reading disorders)

## Key Insights

- **Emotional relief** (rather than knowledge acquisition) is the core interaction driver
- Revealed the dynamic process of "meaning-making" and "self-reconstruction" through AI in non-native environments
- Provided strategic input for cross-cultural adaptation and cognitive sovereignty protection of AI products going overseas
