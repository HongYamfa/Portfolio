<p>I am an anthropologist and digital media researcher examining how people navigate relationships with computational systems across culturally heterogeneous environments.</p>

<p>Drawing on a prior formation in computer engineering — particularly natural language processing, which underlies current generative AI — my work focuses on the cognitive ecology of human–AI interaction: from emotional support encounters and meaning-making practices to the reconfiguration of the self. Through philosophy of language and science and technology studies, I am especially interested in how non-dominant language users engage in self-reorientation when interacting with generative AI, and what this reveals about cognitive sovereignty and epistemic injustice in increasingly mediated worlds.</p>

<p>My research is grounded in long-term ethnographic fieldwork across China, Belgium, Germany, and the United Kingdom, combining technical fluency with phenomenological inquiry. I am completing an MSc in Anthropology at KU Leuven, with an Erasmus+ exchange at Heidelberg University's Institute of Anthropology, and hold an MA in Digital Media from Swansea University.</p>

<p>My conference papers have been presented at the <strong>EASA TAN Annual Conference</strong> (University of the Aegean, 2025) and the <strong>8th Youth Anthropology Conference</strong> (Nanjing University, 2022). I have also served as a Research and Teaching Assistant at South-Central Minzu University, contributing to a National Social Science Fund project on oral history and public health events in China.</p>
